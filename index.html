She of Strangeness

★★★★★ 4.7 out of 5 (600+ reviews)

Sent to a distant world as the first emissary of her people, Whorl is captured and kept as property by the very society she came to understand. Among the mida, a matriarchal culture shaped by ritual, silence, and ancient memory, she is not just a curiosity—she is a challenge to everything they believe.

As Whorl learns their ways, speaks their language, and earns their respect, her presence begins to change the world around her. But belonging comes at a price. Torn between the mission that brought her there and the people who come to care for her, Whorl must choose where her loyalty and her future truly lie.

She of Strangeness is a literary science fiction novel about cultural immersion, gender and power, and the hidden costs of respect. For readers who appreciate deep worldbuilding, complex characters, and the kind of stories that stay with you long after the last page.

Mansinger

★★★★★ 4.6 out of 5 (250+ reviews)

In a world where ancient grudges divide nations and the past never stays buried, three lives collide in ways none of them can escape.

A soldier seeks justice for what was taken. A diplomat fights to prevent war. A woman with a mysterious gift holds the key to a future they may not survive.

As old secrets rise and alliances crack, they must choose between vengeance and harmony knowing the wrong choice could destroy everything.

Mansinger is a literary science fiction novel about conflict, forgiveness, and the fragile hope of building something better than the world that came before.

Deserter

★★★★★ 4.8 out of 5 (500+ reviews)

One soldier comes home to a country that buried its boys and called them dead. He fought for honor. He returns to betrayal.

In a world where truth is buried and heroes are forgotten, one man must decide whether to seek revenge or redemption.

Deserter is a gripping military science fiction novel about loyalty, identity, and the cost of coming home to a world that no longer wants you.

About J. Sheldon Jones

The Writer · The Life · The Worlds

About J. Sheldon Jones

I love creating worlds that exist only in my imagination and populating them with characters I can believe. If my readers can also see them as clearly as I do, I consider that success.

I served in the U.S. Navy as a corpsman and hospital corpsman. For over thirty years I worked as a software engineer, building satellite systems and solving problems that rarely had easy answers.

I

The Writer

I love creating worlds that exist only in my imagination and populating them with characters I can believe. If my readers can also see them as clearly as I do, I consider that success.

J. Sheldon Jones writes literary science fiction that takes seriously the questions most novels sidestep. What happens to a society shaped by centuries of gender imbalance? What does first contact really cost the person sent to make it? What does one soldier owe a country that lied to him? His books do not offer easy answers because the questions do not have any.

II

The Life Behind the Work

J. Sheldon Jones served as a Navy corpsman, working alongside Marines and experiencing more of the world than he ever expected.

After his service, he worked as a printer before earning a degree in computer science. He went on to spend more than thirty years writing software for satellites and ground systems.

Through it all, he continued writing stories.

He and his wife recently celebrated their 57th wedding anniversary. They have two children, and their grandson—born on his birthday—is the best gift he has ever received.

III

Now

Jones lives in Colorado Springs with a view of Pikes Peak from his upstairs window.

He is currently completing his fourth novel.
